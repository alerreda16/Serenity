One of the biggest things people do, or give advice about, in order to improve is warding. Warding is a pretty large influence to the game, able to swing games solely due to the vision it provides for your team; being able to reveal enemies and objectives alike on the map. This guide will help teach you everything you need to know about warding; save the repetitive and generic advice which is just to buy wards.
There are four entrances to each side of the jungle, and depending on which lane you're in also depends on where you should place your ward. You will primarily want to ward the enemy jungle's entrance, which is closest to you, as much as possible. Doing so will usually keep you safe from ganks.
Now, if you’re in top lane, there are two places you should be warding, depending on which side of the map you’re on. If you’re on Blue side you should ward the enemy jungle’s entrance; the tri-brush. If you’re on Red side you should be warding the river, between the river brush and baron pit. Be wary also of lane ganks, although they are not as common. If your opponent is pushing you into your tower, be wary of the side brushes in case their jungler has sneaked into them, you can always place lane wards if need be.
If you’re in the mid lane, there are four possible spots you can ward, again depending on which side you’re on. As the map is symmetrical the two spots for either side is the same, which is just the enemy team’s jungle entrance. Try to communicate with your jungler to ward one side of the river whilst you ward the other, so you don’t have to buy wards for both sides the whole game. If you’re jungler is unable to ward for you, then ward the top side of the jungle and stay on that side as well. Doing so will usually provide you with enough time to react in case of any incoming ganks.
Now, for supports, there are two place you should ward if you’re on Red side, and arguably only one spot you should ward if you’re on Blue. If you’re on Red side, you will want to ward, again, the enemy team’s jungle entrance; the tri-bush, and Dragon. If you’re on Blue side, you will generally only need to ward Dragon. Although, if you’re on Blue side and are only warding Dragon, you may want to ward your mid-bot side jungle entrance, as junglers are able to take a specific route through there in order to come up behind you which will generally be guaranteed kill on most instances.
In summary, the primary places you should be warding is at objectives, and to ward to keep you team safe from ganks in the most effective way possible. There are even additional places you should ward as the game progresses; like Baron, buffs, and jungle choke points, and even in their, or even your own, jungle. This guide is just a general outline but it will help keep you and your team safe from unexpected situation, but in the end the rest up to you, and how you and your team want to play.
